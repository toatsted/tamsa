Legislative Recommendations - 86th Legislative Session

  • TAMSA advocates for meaningful assessments that provide timely and relevant information to teachers, parents, and students. We need to protect our most vulnerable children – learning different, special education, and English Language Learners – who are unduly punished by high stakes testing. TAMSA urges you to take the following actions.
  • 1. Remove all high-stakes from the tests
    • Problem: Denying students a diploma based on a single measure when they have met all other graduation requirements puts them at high risk of poor post-secondary outcomes.
    • Solution: Performance level on state assessments should not be tied to promotion in grades 5 and 8. State mandated assessments should not be tied to high school graduation requirements.
    • Bill Language: House Bill 1321, Landgraf, as filed in the 85th Legislative Session.
  • 2. Test no more than required by federal law
    • Problem: Federal law requires each child be tested every year in grades 3-8 in reading and math, and twice in science. Texas exceeds that with 2 additional writing tests and 1 additional social studies test. In high school, students must be tested once in three subjects: reading, math, and science. Texas exceeds that with an additional history and English test, and two incorporated writing tests.
    • Solution: Writing is best assessed through classroom projects that are evaluated by certified teachers. Texas should eliminate the following tests:
      • 4th grade writing
      • 7th grade writing
      • 8th grade social studies
      • English II EOC
      • U.S. History EOC
      • Writing in English I EOC
    • Bill Language: House Bill 515, VanDeaver as filed in the 85th Legislative Session.
  • 3. Remove the sunset date for the Graduation Committee.
    • Problem: If high stakes are not removed from the testing system, it is imperative to offer an option for students to have an alternative path to graduation that is available to them.
    • Solution: Remove the sunset date from statutory language.
    • Bill Language: Senate Bill 463, Seliger, as filed in the 85th Legislative Session.
  • 4. Replace STAAR with national assessments that provide timely and useful feedback.
    • Problem: Current state assessments are not diagnostic as such they are not assisting our children’s educational environment.
    • Solution: Use diagnostic assessments that are proven age-appropriate and give timely, useful feedback to gauge how children are learning.
      • Grades 3-8: use NRTs, such at Stanford, ITBS or ACT Aspire. Another option is using adaptive tests, such as MAPS, which give immediate diagnostic results that aid instruction.
      • High schools: PSAT/ACT equivalent in 9th or 10th grade, or ACT/SAT/TSI in 11th grade.
      • Texas should not attach high-stakes through use of cut-scores.
    • Bill Language: House Bill 1993, Anderson, as filed in the 85th Legislative Session.
